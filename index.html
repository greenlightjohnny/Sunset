Life @ Sunrise Riverside

District 7, HCMC, Vietnam

A short site about my experience living here, hopefully useful for anyone considering the move

Location

It is located in District 7, which has been growing very rapidly. Many people choose to live here instead of District 1 due to the cheaper housing costs. The roads here are bigger, there is less traffic, and overall much more peace and quite. It's a good balance between living in District 1 and living on the outskirts.

The Neighborhood

The street is technically a dead end, it ends up against the river. It is, however, a large area with a few other smaller upperclass housing devolopments. The area is extremely safe, I have not met a single person who has had any kind of problem.

Commute

There are a growing number of people who live in District 7 and commute to District 1 for work. Before you commit to doing this, I recommend you drive the route from the apartment to your work during both morning and afternoon rush hour traffic. Needless to say, Vietnamese traffic is a thing to experience and not for everyone.

Living

Sunrise Riverside is a huge devolopment. Each building is 21 stories tall. There are currently 10 finished with five more under construction. So what is it like living there?

Lift

The main reason I decided to live here! The gym is awesome, all new with lots of room to expand later. Most importantly, they have a power rack. It's a quality gym. It is located on the ground floor, next to the pool. The gym hours are unknown. I've worked out as late as 11pm and been asked to leave at 9pm other times. Sometimes the front desk is manned, sometimes it is not.

Swim

Yes, it has a pool! Located in the center of the complex, it is outdoors and sorrounded by the complex. Though it is very big, it is not deep. With a max depth of about four feet you won't be doing any diving. Right now there are only about 10 poolside lounge chairs, hopefully they will be getting more.

Park

Parking is really easy, it has a massive underground parking lot. It has 24/7 security and you have to swipe your key card to get in. Once inside you can park close to your building and take an elevator straight up. Make sure you find out if your apartment comes with a motorbike or car parking spot, otherwise you will have to pay extra

Security

They went in heavy with this. Security is all over. There is a guard outside each building entrance. You have to swipe your key card to get into the lobby. You have to swipe your key card use the elevator to up, and you can't go to anyother floor except yours or parking. Outside in the pool area there are numerous security guards patrolling all night. Security cameras all over.

Download

Saving the most important for last. Internet has been decent, running at around 35Mbps (4.4MBps) both up and down. Not the fastest but good enough for Skype video calls and Netflix. It has been reliable, only dropping about once every two weeks for a few minutes. I have a SIM card hotspot just case though.

The Bad

The number one thing you should be aware of is construction noise. Even though the buildings are totally finished from the exterior, it does not mean each apartment is ready to move in. Most are currently just bare concrete shells. When someone buys an apartment they have the interior built just how they want. So you could be living on a nice floor but the apartments around you are not finished, and if they decide to finish the interior you are going to be hearing it all day long for the next two months. There is no way of knowing when this will happen. Even if you are in one of the older buildings, remodeling is so cheap that a new owner will often have it completely redone.

Prices

Rent

Here is where things get fuzzy. Apartments are not rented out directly by the company, instead individuals or other companies buy them and in turn rent them out to others. So there is not set pricings whatsever. Generally, you will be looking at $550 - 800$ a months for at two bedroom apartment. Up to $1,100 for a three bedroom. The price will change depending on the length of your rental, how the apartment is furnished, and the view.

Bills

You will need to pay for water and electric, check to see if internet is included or not. Water is super cheap, my first month's bill came to under $9 with two people living there. Electric is a bit more, my bill was $98 for the month of April. This was due to having at least one A/C going nearly 24/7, sometimes all three A/C units. You could get by with cheaper by opening the balcony door at night or using a fan. If you are not home during the day it would be cheaper as well.
